US Research
4 月 2 日，Anthropic 可解释性团队发表论文，对 Claude Sonnet 4.5 进行内部机制分析。研究者编译了 171 个情绪词汇列表，在模型内部发现了对应的"情绪向量"——这些内部表征不仅存在，而且会可测量地影响模型的输出行为。论文强调这不意味着 Claude 具有"意识"或"感受"，但这些"功能性情绪"确实塑造了模型如何处理信息。该研究引发了对 AI 福利与安全性的广泛讨论。
On April 2, Anthropic's Interpretability team published a paper analyzing Claude Sonnet 4.5's internal mechanisms. Researchers compiled 171 emotion words and found corresponding "emotion vectors" inside the model — these internal representations don't just exist but measurably influence Claude's output behavior. The paper clarifies this doesn't mean Claude "feels" emotions, but these "functional emotions" shape how the model processes information. The research sparked widespread discussion about AI welfare and safety.
为何重要：这是 AI 可解释性领域的一次里程碑式发现。理解大型模型内部的"情绪"表征机制，对于 AI 安全、对齐和未来监管具有重要意义。Anthropic 用机械可解释性方法揭示黑箱内部的运作方式，为整个行业提供了方法论范例。
Why it matters:This is a landmark discovery in AI interpretability. Understanding internal "emotion" representations in large models has profound implications for AI safety, alignment, and future regulation. Anthropic's mechanistic interpretability approach reveals inner workings of the black box, providing a methodological example for the entire industry.
