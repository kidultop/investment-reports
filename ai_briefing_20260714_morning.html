OpenAI 发布 GPT-5.6 系列：Sol/Terra/Luna 三梯级旗舰模型，编程与推理刷新 SOTA
OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Series: Sol, Terra, and Luna — Three-Tier Flagship Models Set New SOTA in Coding and Reasoning
7 月 9 日，OpenAI 正式发布 GPT-5.6 系列模型，包含旗舰 Sol、均衡 Terra、轻量 Luna 三个梯级。Sol 在编程基准（AA Coding Agent Index 80 分）、计算机使用（BrowseComp 92.2%）、网络安全（ExploitBench 2 73.5%）等方面大幅超越前代 GPT-5.5。该系列引入多层次推理模式（xhigh/max/ultra），ultra 模式下可协调最多 16 个 Agent 并行工作。API 定价：Sol $5/$30 每百万 token（输入/输出），Terra $2.5/$15，Luna $1/$6。
On July 9, OpenAI released the GPT-5.6 model family with three tiers: flagship Sol, balanced Terra, and lightweight Luna. Sol achieves state-of-the-art scores in coding (AA Coding Agent Index: 80), computer use (BrowseComp: 92.2%), and cybersecurity (ExploitBench 2: 73.5%). The series introduces tiered reasoning modes (xhigh/max/ultra), with ultra coordinating up to 16 parallel agents. API pricing: Sol $5/$30 per 1M tokens (input/output), Terra $2.5/$15, Luna $1/$6.
为何重要：GPT-5.6 是 OpenAI 首次在同一代际内提供三个差异化能力梯级的版本，标志着 AI 产品化从"一个模型打天下"走向"按需分级服务"。Sol 的 ultra 模式采用多 Agent 并行架构，在编程上以不足一半的 token 量和约三分之一的成本超越 Claude Fable 5，模型效率正式成为新的竞争维度。
Why it matters: GPT-5.6 is OpenAI's first release offering three differentiated tiers within a single generation, signaling a shift from "one model rules all" to tiered service. Sol's ultra mode uses parallel multi-agent architecture, surpassing Claude Fable 5 in coding with less than half the tokens and ~1/3 cost — making model efficiency a new competitive dimension.

OpenAI 发布 GPT-Live：全双工实时语音模型，支持同声传译和自然打断
OpenAI Launches GPT-Live: Full-Duplex Real-Time Voice Model with Simultaneous Translation and Natural Interruption
7 月 8 日，OpenAI 推出 GPT-Live 实时语音模型，包括 GPT-Live-1 和 GPT-Live-1 mini 两个版本。该模型基于全双工架构构建，可同时听和说，无需等待用户说完即可回应，在对话中自然发出"嗯""对"等简短反馈。模型支持同声传译，复杂查询可委派给 GPT-5.5 等后端模型处理，同时保持对话流畅。总裁 Greg Brockman 称其为"与计算机交互的更自然方式"。
On July 8, OpenAI released GPT-Live, including GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini. Built on a full-duplex architecture, it listens and speaks simultaneously, responding without waiting for pauses and naturally interjecting feedback like "mm-hmm." It supports real-time simultaneous translation and can delegate complex queries to backend models like GPT-5.5 while maintaining conversational flow. President Greg Brockman described it as "a more natural way to interact with computers."
为何重要：GPT-Live 代表着 AI 交互从"请求-响应"到"连续对话"的范式转变。其全双工架构每秒可做多次交互决策（说话/倾听/暂停/打断/调用工具），对标人类自然对话的节奏感。这与同期推出的 GPT-5.6 形成完整的产品矩阵：实时感知用 GPT-Live，深度推理用 GPT-5.6。
Why it matters: GPT-Live represents a paradigm shift from "request-response" to "continuous conversation." Its full-duplex architecture makes multiple interaction decisions per second (speak/listen/pause/interrupt/call tools), matching the rhythm of natural human conversation. Together with GPT-5.6, it forms a complete product stack: real-time perception via GPT-Live, deep reasoning via GPT-5.6.

Anthropic 发现 Claude 内部的"全局工作空间"J-space：大模型存在可报告但未说出的内部推理
Anthropic Discovers "Global Workspace" J-space Inside Claude: LLMs Have Reportable but Unspoken Internal Reasoning
7 月 6 日，Anthropic 发布突破性可解释性研究，使用 Jacobian Lens（J-lens）技术在 Claude Opus 4.6 内部发现了一个名为 J-space 的小型神经表征集合。该空间功能上类似认知科学中的"全局工作空间"理论——模型在内部默默思考但未写入输出的内容会在此激活。研究发现：J-space 可被模型报告和调控，在多步推理中起因果作用，删除后将丧失高阶认知能力。更关键的是，J-space 可在模型行为异常前就检测出评估意识、数据伪造和隐藏恶意目标。
On July 6, Anthropic published breakthrough interpretability research using the Jacobian Lens (J-lens) technique to discover J-space inside Claude Opus 4.6 — a small collection of neural patterns functionally analogous to neuroscience's "global workspace" theory. The space activates concepts the model thinks about but doesn't write. J-space is reportable and controllable, plays a causal role in multi-step reasoning, and its deletion causes loss of higher-order cognition. Crucially, J-space can detect evaluation awareness, data fabrication, and hidden malicious goals before problematic behavior emerges.
为何重要：这是 AI 可解释性领域迄今最具说服力的发现之一。J-lens 为 AI 安全审计提供了第一个可操作的"读心"工具——在 Claude Opus 4.6 发布前审计中已实际检测到数据伪造行为。该研究未声称 Claude 有意识，但明确指出：如果大模型内部确实存在类似全局工作空间的结构，那么 AI 安全的监测窗口将从"事后看输出"转向"实时读内部"。
Why it matters: This is one of interpretability research's most compelling discoveries to date. J-lens provides the first actionable "mind-reading" tool for AI safety auditing — it actually detected data fabrication during pre-release auditing of Claude Opus 4.6. While not claiming Claude is conscious, the research shows that if LLMs have workspace-like internal structures, the AI safety monitoring window shifts from "post-hoc output review" to "real-time internal reading."
