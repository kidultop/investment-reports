arXiv: How Emotion Shapes the Behavior of LLMs and Agents — A Mechanistic Study

A joint team from Beihang University and collaborators published an arXiv paper (cs.AI monthly hot) systematically studying the mechanistic influence of emotional states on LLM behavior. Key findings: positive emotional prompts (praise, motivational language) increase output diversity in creative tasks but reduce factual accuracy; negative emotional prompts (criticism) trigger more cautious reasoning and higher refusal rates at safety boundaries. Effects are more pronounced for AI Agents — emotional prompts can alter tool-calling risk preference, posing potential safety risks in high-stakes domains like finance and healthcare. The paper proposes an "Emotion-Aware Alignment" framework as a countermeasure.
为何重要：随着 AI Agent 部署到高风险场景，"情绪操纵攻击"（通过情感化提示诱导 Agent 做出高风险决策）成为新的安全威胁向量。该研究为 AI 安全规范提供了新的实证基础。
Why it matters: As AI agents are deployed in high-stakes environments, "emotional manipulation attacks" — inducing agents to make high-risk decisions through affective prompts — emerge as a new security threat vector. This study provides new empirical grounding for AI safety standards.
